Conservation Movements and Challenges:

India has a rich history of conservation efforts, but significant challenges remain, such as human-wildlife conflict and the need for community involvement in protecting biodiversity. The focus is on restoring habitats and engaging local communities to mitigate issues like crop damage from wildlife​

Government Initiatives:

The Indian government has established numerous protected areas, including national parks and wildlife sanctuaries, under the Wildlife (Protection) Act of 1972. Programs like 'Project Tiger' and 'Project Elephant' aim to support wildlife conservation and habitat protection

Impact of COVID-19:

The pandemic has sparked greater public interest in biodiversity and led to citizen science initiatives that highlight urban biodiversity. This newfound interest presents an opportunity to engage more people in conservation efforts​

Biodiversity and Health:

India is exploring the connections between biodiversity and public health, focusing on how ecosystem health can help manage diseases. Programs are being developed to monitor and understand how biodiversity loss can impact the emergence of zoonotic diseases
