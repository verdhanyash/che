What Is On Land

Earth

Biodiversity on Earth encompasses the variety of life forms across ecosystems, essential for maintaining ecological balance. It supports vital services like nutrient cycling and climate regulation, crucial for human survival.

Forest

Biodiversity in forests includes a wide range of plant and animal species that contribute to ecosystem stability and resilience. These diverse life forms are vital for processes like carbon storage, soil health, and water regulation, supporting both wildlife and human communities.

🌵

Desert

Biodiversity in deserts includes uniquely adapted plants and animals that thrive in extreme conditions, playing vital roles in ecosystem stability. This diversity is crucial for soil health, water conservation, and supporting food webs, helping to sustain both wildlife and human populations.

Ocean

Biodiversity in the ocean consists of a vast array of species, from microscopic plankton to large marine mammals, crucial for maintaining ecosystem health. It supports essential functions like nutrient cycling, carbon storage, and oxygen production, vital for life on Earth.
